# Executive Summary

In this Search Engine Journal interview, Kevin Indig joins Editor-in-Chief Katie Morton to discuss the growing impact of AI Overviews on search visibility, user behavior, and modern SEO strategy. Drawing from Kevin's original AI Overviews research, the discussion explores how marketers should respond to declining organic traffic, increasing brand importance, and the need for cross-functional collaboration.

The conversation emphasizes that SEO is evolving beyond rankings toward a broader discipline that integrates branding, content, product, and marketing strategy.

# Transcript Summary

Katie Morton opens the interview by asking Kevin to explain the key findings from his recent AI Overviews study.

Kevin discusses how AI Overviews are changing user interaction with search results, reducing traditional click-through opportunities while increasing the importance of brand recognition and trust.

The discussion continues by examining how the SEO profession is evolving. Kevin argues that SEO teams should work more closely with product, content, engineering, and marketing departments instead of operating independently.

The interview concludes with practical advice encouraging marketers to diversify traffic sources, embrace experimentation, and adapt to the changing search landscape rather than relying solely on Google's traditional organic search.

# Key Insights

## 1. AI Overviews are changing organic search behavior.

Traditional search traffic is increasingly influenced by AI-generated answers, requiring marketers to rethink performance measurement.

---

## 2. Brand trust has become a competitive advantage.

Recognizable brands receive greater visibility and user confidence within AI-powered search environments.

---

## 3. SEO should become cross-functional.

Successful organizations integrate SEO with product, engineering, content, and marketing rather than treating SEO as an isolated discipline.

---

## 4. Multi-channel visibility reduces dependency on Google.

Brands should develop audiences across multiple platforms to reduce reliance on a single traffic source.

---

## 5. Experimentation is essential.

Organizations should encourage responsible experimentation to adapt more quickly to AI-driven changes.

---

# Actionable Takeaways
